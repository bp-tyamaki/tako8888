Goodbye, 5chan!

The Downfall of an Iconic Japanese Social Media

By YAMAKI, Takayuki

5chan: The Cultural Underground that Even Inspired Films

5chan, once a bustling center of conversation and culture in Japan, is witnessing a dramatic decline. Esteemed for its diverse forums spanning topics from politics to pop culture, 5ch has significantly influenced online dialogue in Japan. Despite its influence, the platform is no longer among Japan’s top visited sites, partly due to ongoing issues with vandalism.

Initially established as a haven for lively, anonymous debates, 5ch provided a unique space where individuals could share their views without disclosing their identity. This anonymity fostered a rich tapestry of interactions, from insightful discussions to heated debates.

Yet, 5chan has always been a double-edged sword. On one hand, it has been at the forefront of Japan’s internet culture, birthing phenomena like the "Train Man" saga and popularizing internet memes among teenagers, such as the catchphrase "Ngo" originated by high school girls. On the other hand, it has also served as a breeding ground for more nefarious activities, including instances of racial discrimination against ethnic Koreans in Japan.

Furthermore, 5chan’s connections extend internationally through figures like Jim Watkins and "Hiroyuki" Nishimura, linking it to Western platforms such as 4chan and 8kun, known for their own controversial reputations. This dual nature of 5ch highlights the complex impact such platforms can have on both national and global scales.
